Workshops

Weight Bench

The weight bench takes bars and allows units to train with them. It will raise their athletic skill, which allows them to train faster. It also raises their physical attributes, making this a good exercise for soldiers that are off duty.

Brick Splitting Block

Has only one reaction: Split a boulder into 10 blocks. An essential buildings for quick aboveground constructions.

Merchant Stall (local)

Merchant stalls are basic workshops that only require blocks to be build. They enable you to sell or buy materials or items. Its very straightforward, you put in items or coins, and get back items or coins.

Weapons, Trapcomps, Clothing and Crafts are a bit special. They have a voucher system, allowing you to buy and sell items of different materials. You first buy vouchers with a specific material, lets say steel. You can then buy items with these vouchers, giving you a steel item. Or you sell items, which gives you vouchers back in their material, enabling you to buy new items made of these materials. If you sell 100 bronze daggers, you get 100 bronze vouchers, enabling you to buy a lot of bronze items.

Workshop (foreign)

Foreign workshops require a contract to be rented out. You can buy a contract from any visiting caravan. It would be best to order lots of them from their liaisons. They come in different prices, sometimes you are lucky, sometimes it might get expensive. They allow you to rent a workshop to that civ for 3 months, netting you an income of 500 gold directly. After that time the workshop will revert back to normal.

Succubus - Summoner

  • For souls and rare ingredients, the succubus summons warbeasts and demons to your side.

Kobold - Potter

  • Clay, fireclay and kaolinite is shaped by the kobold potter into items, which can be burned in your clay oven.

Orc - Artisan

  • Upgrades leather cheaply and grinds rare ores and metals into powder, used as reagents for handcrafted armors and weapons. From lamellar leather armor sets, bone masks, wolfram maces, mithril katanas to cobalt throwing tomahawks.

Goblin - Trapper

  • Makes batches of cages and mechanisms. This costs either 5 logs or 5 boulders for 10 items, which is twice as efficient as the carpenter/mechanic. Produces batches of 5 random trapcomps, or 6 random upright spikes from either obsidian, wood or gems, as well as green glass, clear glass or crystal glass.

Dwarf - Coldforge

  • Forges tin, copper, and bronze items without the need for fuel, directly from ore.

Elf -Treesinger

  • Takes any 5 seeds and 1 soul to weave them together, instantly growing the plants.

Human - Gunsmith

  • Makes pistols, muskets and fitting ammo, from bronze, iron or steel. The weapons cost bars, mechanisms, wood and blackpowder, which is made in the alchemist. Pistols cost 1 bar, muskets 2 and ammo 1.

Guildhall (empty)

A guildhall can be upgraded to a specific guild, allowing your men and women to join a guild. You can also upgrade the guild, which can be done 5 times. First upgrade establishes the guild, and every following update unlocks one additional building, the Guild Extensions. You can buy their permits once you unlocked the fitting guild upgrade.

Prices are 1500 gold for guild membership and 1000 gold for a guild extension permit. Upgrades cost 2000 for the first, 3000 for the second, 4000 for the third and 5000 for the fourth and last. A fully upgraded guild costs 19000 gold coins.

Joining a guild gives the member a 200% skill learn rate. Here the 12 guilds:

  • Masons Guild
  • Carpenters Guild
  • Engineers Guild
  • Farmers Guild
  • Fishers Guild
  • Jewelers Guild
  • Cooks Guild
  • Clothiers Guild
  • Academics Guild
  • Tanners Guild
  • Merchants Guild
  • Smiths Guild

The merchants guild has a unique feature: For each upgrade it allows you to buy 2 new contracts from specific races. When you first build it, you can buy human and dwarven contracts. After the update you unlock elven and gnome, then goblin and kobold, then orc and drow and in the end succubi and warlock contracts. They cost progressively more with each version, but allow you to unlock their workshops without having to rely on caravans. An alternative is the human merchant, who sells random contracts as well.

Guild Extension North

The first guild extension you unlock, these workshops are designed to fit to the northern side of the guildhall. You can build them where ever you like though.

  • Mason - Brick Oven
  • Uses boulders and fuel to create colored blocks. Great for aboveground constructions.
  • Carpenter - Sawmill
  • Cuts logs into planks and refines woods.
  • Engineer - Coin Mint
  • Mints gold, silver or copper into batches of coins. No fuel needed.
  • Farmer - Screw Press
  • Presses extracts and oils from plants, or wood pulp and paper from wood.
  • Fisher - Eel Pond
  • Slow fishing for food.
  • Jeweler - Gemcutter
  • Makes gem furniture and items from rough gems.
  • Cook - Culinary Kitchen
  • Makes cheeses and other food related products.
  • Clothier - Cloth Tailor
  • Makes complete itemsets of shirt, pants and 2 shoes of a specific material.
  • Academic - Soap Maker
  • Creates soap bars from lye and tallow or oil.
  • Tanner - Greater Tanner
  • Tans scales, chitin and shells in addition to normal leather.
  • Merchant - Exchange office
  • Trades copper or silver coins for gold coins.
  • Smith - Crucible
  • Allows the production of bronze and steel, as well as aluminum from bauxite, cobalt, tungstens and mithril.

Guild Extension East

The second extension goes to the eastern side, and usually requires the materials or items produced in the first extension.

  • Mason - Stonecrafter
  • Makes furniture and items from 2 rock blocks.
  • Carpenter - Woodcrafter
  • Makes furniture and items from 2 wooden planks.
  • Engineer - Steam Engineer
  • Makes metal build materials for the steam engine.
  • Farmer - Thatchery
  • Makes wicker blocks from plants, and makes furniture and items from wicker blocks.
  • Fisher - Fish Pond
  • Slow fishing for fish.
  • Jeweler - Glasscutter
  • Creates glass furniture and items.
  • Cook - Brewery
  • Brew specific plants into drinks.
  • Clothier - Weaving Machine
  • Unravel cloth to thread, or clothing back to thread.
  • Academic - Alchemist
  • Creates artificial gems from glasses and metals, and creates bonemold and bloodsteel. These are iron and steel-grade metals made from bone, ash and blood. Also creates wildfire and blackpowder from saltpeter, brimstone, oil and lye.
  • Tanner - Leather Upholstery
  • Create furniture and items from leather.
  • Merchant - Chamber of Trade
  • Calls caravans from all kinds of races for coins. It costs 1500 coins. Necessary to unlock most race specific workshops and merchants.
  • Smith - Weaponry
  • Upgrades weapon quality by one step per weapon.

Guild Extension South

The third extension you unlock fits to the southern end of the guildhall.

  • Mason - Rockforge
  • Create rudimentary weapons and armor from rock.
  • Carpenter - Wood Shredder
  • Destroy wooden objects to get logs and sawdust back.
  • Engineer - Siege Engineer
  • Create catapul and ballista parts as well as ballista ammo.
  • Farmer - War Kennels
  • Train and breed dogs.
  • Fisher - Turtle Pond
  • Slow fishing for turtles/shells.
  • Jeweler - Gemforge
  • Create rudimentary weapons and armor from gems.
  • Cook - Wine and Cheese Cellar
  • Age wines and cheeses to increase their value.
  • Clothier - Dyer Studio
  • Dye all types of cloth. Does change the cloth type, used for color coding your fort.
  • Academic - Transmutation Chamber
  • Transforms one type of metal bar into another. Simple.
  • Tanner - Leather Refinery
  • Upgrade leather, scale and chitin to higher grades.
  • Merchant - Chamber of Diplomacy
  • Calls diplomats of any race, good for stopping wars which might break out with elves or dwarves. Only works after your fort became a barony. It costs 500 gold.
  • Smith - Armory
  • Increases armor quality by one step per item.

Guild Extension West

The fourth and last extension unlocks the highest tier of materials or items the guild can offer. After this there are no more updates.

  • Mason - Archaeologist
  • Identifies and restores fossils, relics and treasures found deep underground.
  • Carpenter - Exotic Woodworker
  • Makes items from rare woods, including good/evil and cavern woods.
  • Engineer - Cannon Engineer
  • Build hand-cannons and wildfire bombs. Wildfire bombs are used in traps, filled with wildfire from the Alchemist.
  • Farmer - Herbalist
  • Mixes plants together to brew poisons used for weapons, traps and ammo.
  • Fisher - Oyster Pond
  • Slow fishing for oysters.
  • Jeweler - Glassforge
  • Create weapons and armor from glass.
  • Cook - Booze Burner
  • Create fuel from alcohol.
  • Clothier - Embroidery Studio
  • Makes cloth-based furniture and embroiders it for even more value.
  • Academic - Inventor
  • Can make copies of foreigns weapons and armors.
  • Tanner - Leather Tailor
  • Creates complete armorsets of different leather types.
  • Merchant - Chamber of Migration
  • Calls migrant waves.
  • Smith - Blast Furnace
  • Quickly smelt large batches of ores into bars.

Scriptorium

Using ink and bookcases, you can write books here. These are required for the library sections, see below.

Library Section

Once you have writting 10 books on a specific topic, you can build a library section and upgrade it for a specific topic. Libraries, once set up, train any worker to a set level of skill, usually around 3-5 levels, in any skill that fits their category. For example the Library of War can train all combat skills to level 3, instantly.

  • Library of War
  • Library of Farming
  • Library of Crafting
  • Library of Metalworking
  • Library of Mind & Body
  • Library of Philosophy
