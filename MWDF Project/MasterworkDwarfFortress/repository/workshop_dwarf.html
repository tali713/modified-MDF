Weight Bench -

A simple workshop that trains attributes. A dwarf can run the training reactions on repeat to raise strength, agility, toughness, spatial sense, kinetic sense and willpower, all attributes that help fighting.

It works the same way as the old vanilla pump-operating trick.

Wood Splitting Block -

Cutting logs into planks gives you 4 wooden blocks that can be used in constructions, but not in workshops. Good for walls and floors.

Decorations

Campfire:

Using wood to build a little fire, you may burn items dropped on it; or you set grass or moss on fire, starting a brush fire on the surface or in the caverns. Great to flush out invisible thieves and necromancers, not so good for the local wildlife.

Carpet:

Decorative only, this workshop takes on the color of the cloth it is build from; use it to line your hallways.

Banner:

Decorative only, this workshops adds a little colorful banner.

Display Stand:

Build from a block and any item, it is used to protect your most valuable items from thieves, build great halls full of your most awesome artefacts or maybe establish your own museum.

WORKSHOPS

Furniture Workshop -

Create entire sets of furniture in this workshop. This will greatly simplify outfitting rooms. You can choose stone, bone stacks, single bones, silver, gold or wood as materials. If you stockpile-link it, it is very easy to color code your fortress.

Bedroom Set: => Bed, Box, Door, Cabinet.

Noble Bedroom Set: => Bed, 2 Boxes, Door, Cabinet, Weaponrack, Armorstand.

Diningroom Set: => 4 Tables, 4 Chairs

Office Set: => Table, Chair, Door.

Graveyard Set: => 4 Coffins, Door.

Storage Set: => 3 bins, 3 barrels.

Gemcutter's Workshop -

You can make furniture from gems here. Just treat this as a mason that uses gems instead of boulders. All reactions need one gem, except the gem anvil, which needs three.

Stonecrafter's Workshop -

Creates any stone item not covered by the mason. This includes beds, bins, barrels and buckets, all of which are produced from blocks. You can also make designated magma-safe furniture, good for magma traps and volcano embarks.

Bonecarver's Workshop -

You can create many items from bone. Treat this building like a mason that uses bones instead of boulders. Bone armor should still be made in the craftsdwarf.

Woodcrafter's Workshop -

Your woodcrafters can use planks to make any wooden item here, which is more economic then the carpenter. You can also make parquet tiles, which can only be used for constructions.

Tailor's Workshop -

The Tailor accepts specific animal materials to create sets of clothing. You can order sets of wool, cotton, fur, silk or rare silk clothing. This costs 3 units of cloth and creates 2 shoes, 1 shirt and 1 trouser.

You can make armor sets from leather, hardened leather, chitin, chitinplate, shell, shellplate, scale, scaleplate. This costs 5 units of leather and gives you 2 boots, 1 trouser, 1 armor and 1 helm.

You can also take cloth and unravel it back into thread, if you need thread for sutures or moods.

Thatchery -

Allows five kinds of plants to be bundled into wicker bales, using one plant for each bale. Wicker items are very light, making it a good material for bins.

Pottery -

The Pottery shapes clay items. You can make clay, fire clay and kaolinite items from the collected raw materials, resulting in greenware items. Greenware is a special name for wet clay.

To make it simpler: Shape Item in Pottery=> Fire Item in Clay Oven.

The material progression is: Clay => Greenware => Ceramic.

ADMINISTRATION

Guildhall -

Guilds are dwarven castes with higher learn rates then normal dwarves. They learn all skills related to their guild twice as fast. There are 12 guilds in total.

Joining a guild costs 2500 gold. Dwarves can also switch guilds or leave them.

Garrison -

The garrison is the military counterpart to the guilds. Military groups are dwarven castes with higher learn rates then normal dwarves. They learn all skills related to their group twice as fast. There are 4 groups in total, you can find more info on them in the "general changes" section.

Joining a group costs 2500 gold. Dwarves can also switch groups or leave them.

The four groups are very distinctive, offensive (Legion), defensive (Guard), ranged (Marksman) and speed (Wrestlers) They are also the only dwarves that can enter martial trances and that get positive thoughts from fighting.

Archeologist's Study -

The Archeologist uses found fossils, relics and treasures to create random rewards. Fossils are single tiles in rock. Ruined brick veins have a higher chance of containing any type of relics and treasure.

Fossils actually count as gems, but please do not cut them in the Jeweller, it makes them unusable to the Archeologist. All reactions are run automatically, your Archeologist will pick up the fossil and choose the right reaction for you.

Some rewards might have a peculiar material or item. Not all things you find are of a beneficial sort.

Alchemist's Laboratory -

You can also creature artificial gems. Take green glass and gold to create emeralds, clear glass and gold for rubies and crystal glass and gold for diamond.

Last part is the transmutation of metals. Take a flux stone as catalyst and two metal bars, to create one bar of the next metal. It is a cycle that allows access to all base metals in the game. You can not create alloys this way, but all reagents needed for alloys.

Libraries train skills. Each of the 6 libraries trains fitting skills instantly to either level 4 to 6, depending on type. Mental skills go to level 6, normal skills to level 5, military skills to level 4.

Good students finish these reactions faster. Use workshop profiles to determine which dwarf you want to train in which library, or just use locked doors.

Scriptorium -

The Scriptorium writes books for the libraries.

Writing books will allow you to build libraries. You will need ink and parchments and book bindings. 10 different books are needed for each library.

Ink => Craftsdwarf, from dye, graphite, blood or ash.

Parchment and book bindings are the normal vanilla DF items

Great Archive -

Build from cabinets and statues, this simple workshop allows you to open legends mode while playing fortress mode.

Look up what that titan or megabeast has been up to before coming to town, or which migrant might be a vampire...

RELIGION

Shrine of Armok -

Shrines are the most basic of religious buildings. Any dwarf can pray at them to receive a small gift from Armok. The success rate is very low, 3-6% for most things. Pray 30 times, get 1 item. But it is free. So if your moody dwarf is about to go berserk, pray for whatever he is missing.

DIPLOMACY

Liaisons Office -

This buildings allows you to spawn caravans, diplomats and migrants. It needs dfhack to run and only works on civilizations that exist in your world. This means you can't force a kobold caravan if you play in a world without kobolds.

Ordering migrants or caravans costs gold coins.

TRADING

Merchant Stalls exchange coins for items. Here you can buy items for coins, mostly gold coins. The better the item, the more coins you have to spend. All merchant stalls need a "trade-license" to be build, which you have to order from your liaison first. You will find it in the "misc" section of the tradescreen, next to the other custom tradegoods.

Olon's Culinary Wonders -

Olon sells food and drinks. All natural plants and resulting drinks are available, as well as water. If you buy drinks you will need to bring your own barrel, pot or bucket. You can also buy meat, raw fish or cheese.

Bomrek's Bargains -

Bomreks sells cheap goods, basic materials and is overall more of a disrespectable sort. I wouldn't lend him money, if you know what I mean. Regardless, you can buy wood and stone, cheaper sorts of gems, wool and cotton, basic metals and all leather types.

Teduk's Fine Goods -

Teduk specializes in rare and valuable items and materials. If you want something expensive, hard to get, for advanced workshop and industries, he is the person to talk to. His prices are extremely high, but well worth the cost. Poisons, rare gems, advanced metals, magical and technological wonders, silk, books... fossils, relics and treasure chests... rare plants, blackpowder, golem or turret parts... Teduk can get you all this and more.

Ore Processor -

Its very simple. You mine "gold ore". You "Process gold ore" and you get "pure gold ore" and some boulders or blocks. The gold ore can be used in the smelter. You could smelt the unprocessed ore, but it would result in less outcome.

Brick Oven -

This building is for megaprojects. You can create bricks of any color you like, just use a boulder to create 4 bricks. Or burn a boulder into 2 fine bricks, which are more valuable. Great for color coding your fortress.

Available Colors:

Crematory -

Great for cleaning up your fort. You can burn all kinds of different items, including body parts and remains, any sort of items and wooden furniture to get some ash or charcoal out of it. Wooden items produce coal for sure, body parts/remains might leave ash, and all smaller items have a small chance for either.

This is mostly done to reduce the amount of items, so it is nor very efficient in creating ash or coal. I advise to build one or two of these next to your refuse piles.

NON-METAL FORGES

Glassforge -

You can create weapons, ammo and armor in this workshop. You need rough glass for the items. Prices range from one to four units of glass per item.

You have three sets: clear, crystal or green glass.

Gemforge -

You can create all weapons and armor with gems, as well as basic ammo. You need wood and gems for the ammo, and several gems for each of the bigger items. The created weapons are extremely sharp. The prices range from 1-4 gems.

Rockforge -

You can create basic weapons and armor with stones, as well as basic ammo. You need several boulders for each of the bigger items. The created weapons are quite heavy. The prices range from 1-4 boulders.

Boneforge -

You can create basic weapons and armor with bones, as well as basic ammo. You several bones for each of the bigger items. The created weapons are quite heavy. The prices range from 1-4 boulders.

METAL PRODUCTION

Blast Furnace & Magma Blast Furnace -

 

The blast furnace has one job and one job only, to make metal bars. You need a big selection of materials to build it, including leather for bellows. It will greatly shorten the amount of time needed for basic metal generation. It cannot handle the more advanced new metals.

Crucible & Magma Crucible -

 

The crucible is a big melting pot to extract base minerals or purify metals. You can remove the rust from iron and steel, or melt blocks into obsidian and slade. Four blocks give your one obsidian, eight blocks for slade.

The metals are chrome, cobalt, mithril, titanium and wolfram. Chrome is decorational only, cobalt around iron-grade, mithril around steel-grade but very light, titanium decorational only, and wolfram very heavy.

The most important reaction is the more efficient making of both bronze and steel the crucible allows. You will get one additional bar, as well as a small chance for another one. It is about 65% more efficient then the smelter reactions.

Finishing Forge -

You can coldhammer tin, copper and bronze here, bars as well as items. This means you can very quickly without the need for fuel create copper or bronze weapons and armors for an early milita. This is designed to bridge the gap between start and steel.

Metallurgist & Magma Metallurgist -

All alloys, except bronze and steel, have been moved to the Metallurgist. I strongly advise to leave this building in the game, to have access to all the vanilla metals.

METAL FORGES

Heavy Siegeworks & Magma Heavy Siegeworks -

The heavy siegeworks allow the creation of ballista ammo directly from metal. No need to make a wooden bolt and attach a head. You can take bars and smelt 3 ballista bolts in one go.

You can also create catapult and ballista parts from metals.

Weaponry/Armory & Magma Weaponry/Armory -

 

Allows to improve the quality of a non-improved weapon or armor by one step. For example a exceptional breastplate will be made into a masterpiece breastplate.

Please do not try to improve masterpiece quality items, because they will lose their quality modifier, due to the game not expecting a higher quality level than the maximum.

Magma-Tech

Volcanic Foundry -

The great magma forge creates the third best material in the game. Only Adamantine is better. It is very, very costly however. To forge volcanic gear, which is warm to the touch, you need to combine obsidian, slade, steel and mithril. The result is the highest achievement of dwarven metalworkers.

You cannot create a Volcanic Foundry without magma.
